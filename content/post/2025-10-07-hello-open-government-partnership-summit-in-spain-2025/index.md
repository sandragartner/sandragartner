I represent Open Knowledge Sweden, meet Open Knowledge Foundation Network and talk about my work at MetaSolutions AB with EntryScape and the Swedish National Data Workshop (Nationell dataverkstad) together with many municipalities, regions and state agencies. I am available to talk about open data, open government, digital sovereignty, metadata, open standards, datamodelling for true interoperability and civictech projects like Handlingar.se featured by Civic Tech Sweden.

The Summit gathers more than 2000(!) high-level representatives of governments, civil society leaders, and policy-makers from around the world to exchange experiences, best practices, and progress on open government initiatives and implementation on key issues.

The event was like the traditional Swedish democracy festival in the start of the summer in Almedalen on the island of Gotland in Sweden.

### Highlights and insights
- An overwhelming support for digital sovereignty.
- The support for open government is growing in pro-democracy countries.
- Meeting Swedish central national government about the Open Government Partnership and representing civil society via the non-profit association Open Knowledge Sweden.
- There is very much a need for more open government to enable government efficiency and reduce corruption.
- Sweden is not viewed as a leader in transparency anymore, barely anyone talked about Sweden.
- Many countries in Africa, Asia and Latin America are making great strides towards increasing open government.
- Data needs to be rejuvenated with interoperability to enable more value and seamless magical experiences.
